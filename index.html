Is a service dog right for you?

What you need to consider: A service dog can expand your life, but it is complicated. You need to make sure that you are eligible to partner with a service dog, that you can properly care for a dog, and that the benefits of having one will outweigh the negatives. (Yes, there are some drawbacks.)

Are you eligible?
Only someone with a genuine disability can have a service dog. That means a physical or other disability that interferes with your Activities of Daily Living. You can get more information on that here: ADL Disability

Can you care for a dog?
Dogs rely 100% on their people to give them everything they need: a safe and comfortable home, healthy food, daily exericise, veterinary care, and so on. It can be expensive and takes time and energy. What resources (financial and in social support) do you have available?

What could a service dog do for you?
Service dogs specialize in mitigating specific disabilities, from blindness and hearing loss to diabetes, epilepsy, weakness and balance difficulties, PTSD, Alzheimers, and more. You can see lists of service dog tasks here: Service dog task list

If you have a disability, a service dog can help. Seek out a professional for advice.
